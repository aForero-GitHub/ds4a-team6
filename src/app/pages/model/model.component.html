Random Forest

Everything started from understanding the context and analyzing Christus Sinergiaâ€™s objective: predicting, based on risk factors, which patients would develop hypertension and which would not, That is, we wanted a binary result: the patient will suffer from hypertension or not, based on qualitative and quantitative categorical factors and non-categorical, numerical and continuous factors.

Based on these characteristics, a Binomial Logistic Regression model was designed and trained, taking into account the Gender, BMI, Age, Weight, Glucose, Triglycerides and Total Cholesterol factors. This model allowed us to identify the significant variables to explain the final diagnosis. However, we found that the model was not entirely reliable.

We then decided to develop a Random Forest algorithm that would take into account these variables, already defined as significant, but that would allow us to have a higher level of certainty. After several iterations, we decide this will be the model used to predict hypertension diagnoses in Christus Sinergia patients.
